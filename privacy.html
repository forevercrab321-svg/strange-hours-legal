Strange Hours

Privacy Policy

Last updated 3 July 2026

Strange Hours is an automated folklore-horror storytelling channel. This policy explains exactly what our TikTok integration ("the App") touches — and, more to the point, what it doesn't.

Who this covers

The App is used solely by the owner of the Strange Hours accounts to publish our own original videos to our own TikTok account. It does not serve, target, or collect data from any third-party end users.

What the App accesses

Through the TikTok Content Posting API and Login Kit — and only with the account owner's explicit authorization — the App uses:

  • user.info.basic — to confirm the connected account is ours.
  • video.upload / video.publish — to upload and publish videos we created ourselves, with captions and cover images we wrote.

We upload only our own finished content. We never read, collect, or store other people's videos, followers, messages, or personal information.

Access tokens

OAuth access and refresh tokens are stored locally on the owner's own computer in a permission-restricted file (chmod 600). They are never sold, shared, transmitted to third parties, or exposed publicly, and are used only to publish our own content.

Retention & deletion

We keep only what publishing requires — the video files, captions, and post-confirmation IDs — stored locally. Authorization can be revoked at any time from TikTok's connected-apps settings, which immediately ends all access.

Third parties

We do not sell, rent, or share data with third parties. Video generation uses the MiniMax API on our own content only.

Children

Neither the content nor the App is directed to children under 13.
